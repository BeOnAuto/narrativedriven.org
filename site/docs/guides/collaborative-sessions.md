# Running a Collaborative NDD Session

![Five speech bubbles around a central narrative card, two bubbles holding a code-bracket and a gear](/images/heroes/collaborative-sessions.png){.page-hero}

NDD sessions are collaborative modeling sessions around a shared product story. The taxonomy gives the room a common language: domain, narrative, scene, moment, rule, example, and should statement.

*This guide is being written. In the meantime, here's the essential structure:*

## Invite the right people

You need complementary knowledge. Someone who knows the business rules, someone who knows the technical constraints, someone who knows the users. Three to five people is ideal.

## AI in the room

In the AI era, your session may include AI as a drafting assistant. The group can start from a draft narrative, then refine it together.

This changes the facilitation dynamic. You're not starting from a blank wall. You're starting from a draft and using the group's collective knowledge to make it right.

## The process

1. **Name the domain.** What business capability are you modeling? Capture the actors and shared language.
2. **Identify the narratives.** What broader goals does this domain need to fulfill? Each becomes a goal thread.
3. **Draft the first version.** Describe what you're building. Use the NDD taxonomy to shape the first pass.
4. **Read it aloud.** Walk the narrative scene by scene, moment by moment. Does it flow? Does each scene name a real outcome?
5. **Challenge the rules.** Are the Given/When/Then examples concrete enough? What edge cases are missing?
6. **Check screen content.** Does every important field have an explained product source?
7. **Note new outcomes.** Edge cases that take the actor to a meaningfully different state become their own scenes.

## Tips

**Start with the primary outcome of each narrative.** It's tempting to dive into edge cases first. Get the main outcome modeled, then add sibling scenes for the alternative outcomes.

**Read the narrative aloud.** Your brain is wired to detect gaps in a story. An incomplete narrative will sound wrong. The group will instinctively want to fix it.

**Use document review for mixed groups.** Non-technical participants are usually most comfortable reading the narrative as prose. Visual review helps when the team needs to reason about flow.

NDD can also be practiced with Miro boards or physical whiteboards. The concepts are the same.
