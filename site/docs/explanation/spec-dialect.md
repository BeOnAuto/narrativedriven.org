# NDD as a Spec Dialect

![SPEC document card with checklists, code-bracket, and Given/When/Then sections](/images/heroes/spec-dialect.png){.page-hero}

Narrative-Driven Development is a language for describing software intent.

The taxonomy is domains, narratives, scenes, moments, moment types, rules, examples, and should statements.

## What makes NDD a dialect?

A dialect gives teams shared words and repeatable patterns.

NDD gives teams:

- a four-level hierarchy: Domain -> Narrative -> Scene -> Moment
- four moment types: command, query, react, experience
- Given/When/Then examples for business rules
- should statements for interaction and component expectations
- review principles for outcomes, cohesion, and screen content

That is enough for teams to write clearer product intent.

## The practical use

Anyone can use NDD vocabulary in a document, workshop, issue, or prompt.

## Beyond line-of-business

NDD is purpose-built for workflows, forms, dashboards, and orchestration-heavy products. Other domains may need other dialects.

See [specdriven.com/dialects](https://specdriven.com/dialects/) for the broader catalog.
