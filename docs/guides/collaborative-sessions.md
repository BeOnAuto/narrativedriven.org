# Running a Collaborative NDD Session

NDD sessions aren't the whiteboard workshops of the past. They're collaborative modeling sessions where humans and AI work together on a shared model. Here's how to run one effectively.

*This guide is being written. In the meantime, here's the essential structure:*

## Invite the Right People

You need complementary knowledge. Someone who knows the business rules, someone who knows the technical constraints, someone who knows the users. Three to five people is ideal.

## AI in the Room

In the AI era, your session likely includes AI as a participant. On the Auto platform, you can prompt the AI to generate an initial model, then the group refines it together. The AI generates narratives, scenes, moments, and business specs. The humans validate, challenge, and adjust.

This changes the facilitation dynamic. You're not starting from a blank wall. You're starting from an AI-generated draft and using the group's collective knowledge to make it right.

## The Process

1. **Start with the outcome**: What business value does this narrative deliver?
2. **Generate the first draft**: Describe what you're building. AI generates the initial model.
3. **Read it aloud**: Go through the narrative scene by scene, moment by moment. Does it flow? Does it make sense?
4. **Challenge the specs**: Are the Given/When/Then examples concrete enough? What edge cases are missing?
5. **Check data completeness**: Can you trace every piece of state back to a command?
6. **Note side narratives**: Edge cases and alternate flows become separate scenes or narratives. Don't dismiss them; capture them for later.

## Tips

**Keep focused on the happy path first.** Edge cases and error flows are important, but they're separate scenes. Don't let them derail the main narrative.

**Read the narrative aloud.** Your brain is wired to detect gaps in a story. An incomplete narrative will sound wrong. The group will instinctively want to fix it.

**Use the document view.** Non-technical participants are most comfortable in the document view. Developers can follow along in the code view simultaneously.

If you want to run a more traditional facilitation session (without the Auto platform), NDD can be practiced with Miro boards or even physical whiteboards. The concepts are the same; you just lose the model-level benefits of bidirectional sync and AI assistance.
